Research Highlights

My research lies in closing the loop between AI and scientific discovery and building biomedical and healthcare AI applications.
I believe that building robust and generalizable bio/healthcare AI relies on learning physically reasonable models by understanding the data generation process of the observable data (e.g., the generative causal process for the physical system) and the underlying inherent structures and topology of data (e.g., general graph learning and incorporating structural priors for learning).
To achieve this goal, I work on these directions:

  • ➤ Structural or causal representation learning for scientific discovery: Discover the underlying physical process for molecular dynamics under various environments [NeurIPS’22 workshop Oral].
  • ➤ General graph learning: Improve generalization and robustness of graph models via disentangle learning and pre-training [Under review], graph-structured neural networks [PR’23].
  • ➤ Healthcare and medical AI applications: Efficient Bio-mechanical parameter estimation [JoB’20; EMBC’23]; Drug discovery [EMBC’20]
